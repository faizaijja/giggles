History Treehouse: Rwanda's Story

‚è∞ Time Safari: Rwanda's Past

Rwanda has a fascinating history! Let's travel back in time to learn about this beautiful country.

  • 1884-1919: Germany controlled Rwanda
  • 1919-1962: Belgium controlled Rwanda
  • July 1, 1962: Rwanda gained independence!
  • 1994: A very sad time called the genocide against the tutsi happened
  • Since 1994: Rwanda has been rebuilding and growing stronger

Some important people in Rwanda's history:

King Kigeli IV Rwabugiri - Notable King of Rwanda
President Paul Kagame - Leader since 2000

Geography Treehouse: Rwanda's Land

Safari Map: Where is Rwanda?

Rwanda is a small country in East Africa, just south of the Equator.

Rwanda Facts

  • Capital: Kigali
  • Size: 26,338 square kilometers (about the size of Maryland)
  • Population: About 13 million people
  • Nickname: "Land of a Thousand Hills"

Amazing Landscapes of Rwanda

Rwanda has beautiful and diverse landscapes! Let's explore them:

Volcanoes National Park - Mountain gorillas live here!
Lake Kivu - One of Africa's Great Lakes
Nyungwe Forest - Home to many monkeys and birds
Rolling Hills - Why Rwanda is called "Land of a Thousand Hills"

Make Rwandan Banana Bread!

Rwanda grows many types of bananas. Try this simple recipe:

Ingredients:
  • 3 ripe bananas
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
Instructions:
  1. Mash bananas in a bowl
  2. Mix in eggs, oil, and sugar
  3. Add flour, baking soda, and salt
  4. Pour into a greased pan
  5. Bake at 350¬∞F (175¬∞C) for 45-50 minutes

Rwanda has strong values that guide how people live together.
